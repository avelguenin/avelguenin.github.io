Creation

I have spend the last few years building the outline of an uchronical fiction on spirituality, power, and history. It is constituted by a web of related stories collectively drafting the progressive institution of a neognostic Temple of Tyr as an hegemonic geopolitical power and its consequent desintegration into internal warfare. Each story captures how a specific actor participates in this trajectory, and seeks to convey how their particular perspective derive from but also participate in shaping historical dynamics beyond their all too human compehension.

The central goal of this project is to convey at an intimate level how power derives ultimately from the renunciation to control. All characters become entangled to some level in the Temple’s affairs, and their unbalances are turend into leverages to bring them into the role they are expected to fulfill. The Temple’s institutions, the strategies and attitudes of its militant, the esoteric doctrine they practice and the exoteric doctrine they prache progressively reveal its underlying ethics, which holds the ability to shape the material world through spiritual power as the only kind of virtue.

Exerpts will be published as I see fit.
