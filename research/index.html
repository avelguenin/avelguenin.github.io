Research

My main interest is to study the dynamics of creative evolution and multi-scale organization in complex adaptive systems, such as woodlice and human societies. The core insight I draw upon is that such system are constrained by their material organization, their history, and the context of those operation, but ultimately lack a “natural” state space enabling a full representation of their possible states of organization. Therefore, they are poised on the verge of a space of adjacent possibles, as their state space continuously evolves as a result of their own activity.

This basic constatation motivate the formulation of multiple, although deeply interwoven, questions.

  • Epistemologically, what does it mean to measure and explain features of creative evolution ?
    • Guénin–Carlut, Avel. 2020. ‘Cognition in Eco, Cognition in Vitro - Measurement and Explanation in Cognitive Science’. https://osf.io/ercz6/ (February 11, 2022).
  • Sociologically, how do new forms of organization emerge in human history, and how do they constraint future evolution ?
    • *Guénin—Carlut, Avel. 2022. “Thinking like a State : Embodied Intelligence in the Deep History of Our Collective Mind.” IOP Conference Series: Materials Science and Engineering 1261 (1): 012026. https://doi.org/10.1088/1757-899X/1261/1/012026.
  • Computationally, how and why model the dynamics of creative evolution in collective systems ?
    • Guénin–Carlut, Avel. 2022. ‘Cognitive Agency in Sociocultural Evolution’. https://osf.io/x7yr4/ (January 14, 2022).
  • Formally, how to represent creative evolution ?
    • Guénin–Carlut, Avel. 2022. “Physics of Creation - Symmetry Breaking, (En)Active Inference, and Unfolding Statespaces.” OSF Preprints. https://doi.org/10.31219/osf.io/68947.

Ongoing projects

  • From the existential stance to social constraints. Research project. Role: Research coordinator. Goal: Exposing the role of embodied synchronisation and explicit theory of mind in the development of social constraints (such as norms or affordances), and their ability to enact social regulation.
  • Active Inference and collective intelligence. Active Inference Institute community project. Role: Scientific advisor. Goal: Developping an Active Inference approach to the nature and development of intelligence in collective systems, such as neuron network or human collectives.
