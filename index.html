About me

I’m looking to get into a PhD contract centered on modelling creative evolution in collective systems. Any help on this would be greatly appreciated.

I am a complex systems researcher who participates in the construction of an integrated conception of life, cognition, and culture as an enactive dynamical process across scales. I draw from both philosophical and computational tools to understand how and why complex functional entities assemble in living systems, and what it means to exist as such a unit. I seek to discover why particular systems create meaning, how this process is embedded in the evolution of human societies, and what it means for our role as situated humans in shaping the collective future.

I want to participate in building societies that will withstand the test of the so-called Anthropocene era, which I extend my research work beyond classical (academic) institutions. I am developing the Kairos participative research & scientific mediation collective which focuses on synthesising complexity-oriented approaches to social sciences. I also work on a cooperative project aimed at integrated synergic research-action approaches to build governance systems and actors network that can navigate real world complexity.

I have a broader commitment to reveal what’s below sensible reality and beyond the means of our linguistic grasp. This is why I’m interested in various ways to convey meaning without the need for formal language, from (classical) graphical design to creative ventures. I am especially interested in exploring the ethics of impermanence through graphism and fiction. I write stories exploring how actors struggling to impose meaning onto a shifting and ultimately inaccessible reality, and how this process ultimately shapes the evolution of human societies.

You can find me on OSF, Github, or contact me by mail. If you like my work or I helped you in some way, please consider making a donation via LiberaPay or Ko-Fi.
